Nicholas Barbara
PhD Candidate in Robotics & Control
Australian Centre for Robotics | University of Sydney

Engineer and researcher with a passion for solving challenging problems and understanding how the world works. I’m currently completing my PhD at the Australian Centre for Robotics (ACFR), where my research involves developing new robust machine learning tools for applications such as learning-based robotic control.


I'm most interested in working at the intersection of science and engineering. At the moment, my focus is on control theory, reinforcement learning, neural networks, and robotics. I also dabble in astronomy and space engineering. In my spare time, I love a little bit of cricket and jazz.


To get in touch, please feel free to reach out via email at nicholas.barbara@sydney.edu.au.


LinkedIn Google Scholar GitHub

Background

I received a Bachelor of Engineering (Hons 1, University Medal) & Bachelor of Science (Advanced) from the University of Sydney in 2021. My majors were in Space Engineering, Physics, and Mathematics. I completed my honours thesis in vibration control as a visiting researcher at Nearmap, and had previously interned at Saber Astronautics and ISAE-SUPAERO. Before that, I was a student researcher at the Sydney Institute for Astronomy (SIfA) where I developed a data-driven classification pipeline for asteroseismology.
